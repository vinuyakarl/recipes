Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a classic and beloved Italian pasta dish that has become popliar worldwide. It consists of long, thin noodles made from wheat flour, water, and sometimes eggs. The pasta is cooked until it reaches a tender yet slightly firm texture, known as "al dente." Spaghetti is often paired with a variety of sauces, with the most common being tomato-based sauces like marinara or Bolognese. It is also commonly served with garlic and olive oil, known as aglio e olio, or with creamy sauces like Alfredo. Spaghetti is versatile, allowing for endless variations and combinations with various ingredients such as vegetables, meats, and seafood. It is enjoyed for its comforting and satisfying nature, making it a go-to choice for family dinners, casual gatherings, and even elegant occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225g) spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 can (14 ounces/400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Steps:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion.
  3. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir in the dried basil, dried oregano, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Simmer the sauec for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss until the pasta is well coated.
  5. Remove from heat and serve the spaghetti in individual plates or bowls.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired, and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.
  7. Enjoy your delicious homemade spaghetti!
